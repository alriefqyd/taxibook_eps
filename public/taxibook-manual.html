Overview & Getting Started

Introduction, roles, installation, and login

1. What is TaxiBook EPS?

TaxiBook EPS is PT Vale Indonesia's internal company fleet booking system. It allows employees to request taxi trips within the company site, enables drivers to manage and execute those trips, and gives coordinators full visibility and control over the entire fleet — all in real time.

👤
Staff (Passenger)
Employees who book taxi trips for business travel within the PT Vale site.
🚗
Driver
Company taxi drivers who receive, accept, and execute bookings. GPS location is tracked live.
📋
Coordinator
Fleet supervisors who approve bookings, monitor the fleet, and manage drivers and locations.
🖥️
Dispatch Board
A full-screen real-time dashboard for control rooms, showing the full fleet schedule and map.

2. Install the App (PWA)

TaxiBook EPS is a Progressive Web App — no app store needed. Install it directly from your mobile browser.

Android (Chrome)

1
Open Chrome
Navigate to the TaxiBook EPS URL provided by your admin.
2
Tap the menu (⋮)
Tap the three-dot menu in the top-right corner.
3
Add to Home Screen
Tap "Add to Home screen" and confirm. The TaxiBook icon will appear on your home screen.

iOS (Safari)

1
Open Safari
Navigate to the TaxiBook EPS URL. Must use Safari — not Chrome.
2
Tap Share (↑)
Tap the share icon at the bottom of the screen.
3
Add to Home Screen
Scroll down and tap "Add to Home Screen", then tap Add.
💡
Enable NotificationsWhen prompted, allow push notifications so you receive real-time booking alerts and trip reminders even when the app is in the background.

3. Login

All users share the same login screen. Your role (Staff, Driver, or Coordinator) is determined by your account and assigned by the admin — you do not select it manually.

1
Enter your work email
Use the email address registered by your PT Vale administrator.
2
Enter your password
Contact your admin if you have forgotten your credentials.
3
Tap Sign in
You will be redirected automatically to the correct home screen for your role.

4. Booking Statuses

Every booking moves through a series of statuses from creation to completion. The table below explains each one.

Status Label Meaning Next Step
Submitted Booking has been created by staff and is waiting for processing. System assigns a driver automatically, or coordinator reviews.
Needs Approval This booking requires coordinator review before it can proceed. Coordinator must Approve or Reject.
Awaiting Driver A taxi has been assigned and the driver has been notified. Driver must Accept or Decline within the time limit.
Booked Driver has accepted. The trip is confirmed. Driver starts the trip at the scheduled time.
On Trip The trip is currently in progress. Driver is en route. Driver marks the trip as Waiting or Completed.
✓ Completed Trip is finished. Recorded with actual completion timestamp. —
Rejected Coordinator or system rejected the booking. Staff may re-submit or contact coordinator.
Cancelled Booking was cancelled before the trip started. —

Staff Guide

Booking trips, tracking your ride, and managing notifications

STAFF
Who is this for?

Employees of PTVI especially under EPS department who need to request a company taxi for business travel within or around the site.

5. Home Screen

After login, Staff sees their personal booking dashboard. The home screen shows:

  • Active trip card — if you have an ongoing trip, it appears prominently at the top with a live map button.
  • Upcoming bookings — confirmed trips scheduled for today and the near future.
  • Recent history — past completed or cancelled trips.
  • Quick actions — Book a Trip button prominently available.
💡
Real-time updatesBooking cards update automatically without refreshing the page. Status changes (e.g. "Driver Accepted") appear live.

6. How to Book a Trip

1
Tap "Book a Trip"
From the Home screen or the Book tab in the bottom navigation bar.
2
Enter Pickup Location
Type your pickup address, or choose a saved PT Vale site location from the list. Using saved locations avoids GPS errors inside the plant.
3
Enter Destination
Type your destination or select from saved locations.
4
Set Date & Time
Choose the date and time you need the taxi. You can book in advance.
5
Select Trip Type
Choose DROP for a one-way trip, or WAITING if the driver should wait for you. For WAITING, set the estimated wait time in minutes.
6
Add Notes (optional)
Enter any special instructions for the driver or coordinator.
7
Submit
Tap Submit. You will receive a push notification when your booking is confirmed and a driver is assigned.

7. Trip Types Explained

TypeWhen to useDriver behaviour
DROP One-way journey. You don't need the driver to wait. Drops you off and the trip ends immediately.
WAITING You need the driver to wait at the destination (e.g., a meeting with a return trip). Drops you off, waits for the specified minutes, then you return. The booking spans the full duration.
⚠️
Booking lead timeAlways book at least 15 minutes before your required departure time to allow the system to assign and notify a driver.

Driver Guide

Receiving, accepting, and executing company taxi trips

DRIVER
Who is this for?

EPS department drivers assigned to a company taxi unit, responsible for picking up and transporting PTVI employees for business travel within and around the site.

9. Driver Home — Trip Queue

After logging in, drivers see their personal trip dashboard:

  • Active trip — the current in-progress trip with map and controls.
  • Upcoming trips — accepted bookings scheduled for later today.
  • Pending requests — new bookings waiting for your Accept/Decline response.

The screen updates in real time whenever a new booking is assigned to your taxi.

⚠️
Keep the app openFor GPS tracking to work and for instant booking alerts, keep TaxiBook EPS open or installed as a PWA on your device during working hours.

10. Accept or Decline a Booking

When a new trip is assigned to your taxi, you receive a push notification and the booking appears in your home screen as a pending request.

1
Review the booking details
Check the pickup point, destination, scheduled time, and trip type (DROP or WAITING).
2
Tap Accept or Decline
If you can fulfil the trip, tap Accept. The passenger is notified immediately. If you cannot, tap Decline — the system will attempt to reassign to another available driver.
💡
Repeated declines are trackedThe coordinator can see how many trips you have declined today. Excessive declines may trigger a coordinator review.

11. Running a Trip

1
Tap "Start Trip"
When you depart to pick up the passenger, tap Start Trip. The booking status changes to On Trip. The passenger can now see your live location on their map.
2
Drive to destination
Your GPS position updates automatically every few seconds. Keep the app open and the screen on for best tracking accuracy.
3
For WAITING trips — tap "Arrived / Waiting"
When you reach the destination and the passenger exits, tap this button. Status becomes Waiting and the timer starts.
4
Tap "Complete Trip"
Once the trip is fully done (passenger delivered, or waiting period complete), tap Complete Trip. The booking records the actual finish timestamp and closes.

Driver Guide — GPS & Map

Location tracking and the active trip map

DRIVER

12. GPS Tracking

TaxiBook EPS tracks your vehicle's position automatically when you have an active trip. This location is shared with the passenger and with the coordinator's fleet map.

📱 GPS Requirements
  • Enable Location Services on your device and allow TaxiBook EPS to access your location.
  • For Android: set location permission to "Allow all the time" for background tracking.
  • GPS accuracy is best outdoors. Inside buildings, accuracy may be reduced.
  • Your last known position is stored — if GPS signal is lost briefly, the last known location is shown.

Driver Map View

During an active trip, the driver home screen shows a live map with:

  • Your position — updated every few seconds
  • Route line — road-following route from your current position to the destination (powered by OpenRouteService)
  • Destination marker — clearly labeled with the address
💡
Wake LockThe app keeps your screen active during a trip to prevent GPS from stopping. Your device may use more battery — keep it charged or plugged in during long trips.

Driver Profile

  • Your name and contact information

Coordinator Guide

Managing bookings, fleet, and approvals

COORDINATOR
Who is this for?

EPS department coordinators responsible for overseeing the company fleet, approving trip requests, monitoring all drivers in real time, and ensuring smooth daily transport operations across the PTVI site.

13. Coordinator Home — Booking Management

The coordinator home screen is a full dispatch dashboard with three view tabs at the top:

TabDescription
BookingsList of all bookings with status filters, date range selector, and approval actions.
CalendarGantt-style visual schedule of all taxi trips (Day / Week / Month views).
MapLive fleet map embedded directly in the home screen.

Bookings Tab — Filters

All Pending Confirmed Done

Use the date range picker to filter bookings by scheduled date. A Today shortcut resets to today's date. Use Load more to paginate through older bookings.

14. Approve & Reject Bookings

Some bookings require coordinator approval before a driver can be assigned. These appear in a highlighted section at the very top of the Bookings tab with an orange badge showing the count.

1
Review the booking card
Check the passenger name, pickup, destination, time, and trip type.
2
Tap Approve or Reject
Approving forwards the booking to driver assignment. Rejecting cancels the booking — you may optionally enter a reason.
3
Reassign Taxi (if needed)
Tap the "🔄 Reassign taxi" button on a confirmed booking to move it to a different available driver.

15. Calendar View (Gantt)

Switch to the Calendar tab to see a visual timeline of all trips. The calendar has three view modes:

📅 Day View
Horizontal Gantt. Each row is a taxi. Each block is a booking. Scroll left/right through hours 07:00–19:00. The red "Now" line shows the current time.
🗓️ Week View
One column per day of the week. Each taxi row shows bookings as color-coded blocks. Useful for spotting busy days.
🗓️ Month View
Traditional monthly calendar with colored dots per booking. Tap a day to drill down into Day View.
✓ Completed Trips
Completed trips appear as muted gray blocks on the Gantt showing the actual finish time (Done HH:mm). Only visible on the coordinator calendar.

Coordinator Guide — Fleet & Locations

Live fleet map, driver management, and saved locations

COORDINATOR

16. Fleet Map View

The Map tab on the coordinator home, and the dedicated Fleet Map page (accessible from the bottom navigation), both show a live overhead map of all company taxis.

🗺️ What you see on the Fleet Map
  • Driver markers — each taxi shown as a color-coded pin at their current GPS position.
  • Route lines — for on-trip drivers, a road-following route line (in the taxi's color) runs from the driver to their current destination.
  • Destination labels — a pill label shows the taxi name at the destination point so routes don't get confused.
  • Stale GPS — drivers whose location has not updated in 10+ minutes appear at reduced opacity.

Fleet Map — Driver Board Panel

A floating panel at the bottom of the map shows a scrollable list of all drivers:

  • Color-coded border matching the taxi color
  • ● Online / ○ Offline status (based on driver assignment and availability)
  • GPS active indicator (green satellite icon)
  • For on-trip drivers: pickup → destination route shown as a colored row

The header pills show a live count: X online · Y offline · N GPS active.

17. Driver Management

Access the Drivers page from the bottom navigation to see all registered drivers and taxis.

  • View each driver's name, assigned taxi, and current availability status.
  • See trips completed today and number of declines (a high decline count is highlighted in red as a warning).
  • Toggle a taxi's available/unavailable status directly from this screen.
⚠️
Marking a taxi unavailableWhen you mark a taxi as unavailable, it will no longer receive new booking assignments. Use this when a vehicle is under maintenance or the driver is off duty.

18. Saved Locations

The Saved Locations feature lets coordinators register named places within the PT Vale site. These locations appear as selectable options when staff book a trip, preventing GPS geocoding errors inside the plant area.

Opening Saved Locations

From the coordinator home, tap your avatar (top-right corner) → select "Saved Locations" from the dropdown menu.

Page Layout

The Saved Locations page uses a side-by-side layout:

🗺️ Left — Interactive Map
  • All saved locations are shown as orange ★ star markers on the map.
  • Tap any marker to see a popup with the location name, address, and exact GPS coordinates.
  • Use the ⊞ fit button (top-right of the map) to zoom out and show all pins at once.
  • Standard pinch-to-zoom and drag gestures work as expected.
📋 Right — Location List
  • All saved locations listed as cards, sorted by name.
  • Each card shows the location name, address, and GPS coordinates.
  • Tap a card to fly the map to that location's marker.
  • Use "Load more" at the bottom of the list to page through locations when there are many.

Adding a New Location

1
Tap "+ Add Location"
The button appears at the top of the list panel. A full-screen map picker opens.
2
Pin the exact spot
Tap anywhere on the map to drop the pin, or type an address in the search bar to jump to a location. The pin shows the exact coordinates that will be saved.
3
Enter a name
Give the location a clear, descriptive name — e.g., "Gerbang Utama", "Klinik Sorowako", "Warehouse Gate 3". Staff will see this name when booking.
4
Tap Save
The location is saved instantly and appears on the map and list for all users.

Editing or Deleting a Location

Each card in the location list has a ⋮ three-dot menu button in the top-right corner of the card. Tap it to open a dropdown with two options:

✏️
EditOpens the location picker so you can move the pin and rename the location. Save to apply.
🗑️
DeletePermanently removes the location. It will no longer appear as an option when staff book trips.
ℹ️
Why Saved Locations matterInside the PT Vale plant, GPS-based address lookup (geocoding) is unreliable and can return incorrect road addresses. Using pre-pinned saved locations gives staff accurate pickup and drop-off points with exact coordinates — no guesswork needed.

First-time setupWhen you first open TaxiBook EPS, you will be prompted to allow notifications. Tap Allow. If you accidentally tapped Block, go to your browser/device Settings → Site Permissions → Notifications → enable for the TaxiBook EPS URL.

22. Auto-Complete & Reminders

The system runs automated jobs to handle trips and reminders without manual intervention:

⏰ Auto-Complete
If a driver forgets to mark a trip as completed, the system automatically closes it after the expected end time has passed. A notification is sent to both parties.
🔔 15-Minute Reminder
Sent to both staff and driver 15 minutes before the scheduled departure time, so everyone is prepared.
🚨 Start Reminder
Sent to the driver at exactly the scheduled departure time if the trip has not yet been started.
⚠️ Overdue Alert
If a trip is significantly past its start time and still not started, the coordinator is alerted so they can intervene.
💡
Coordinator: Run Cron ManuallyFrom the coordinator home, the overflow menu includes a "Run Cron" option to trigger the automation cycle immediately — useful for testing or forcing reminders outside the normal schedule.

Glossary

Key terms used throughout TaxiBook EPS

23. Terms & Definitions

TermDefinition
BookingA single taxi trip request created by a staff member, containing pickup, destination, date/time, and trip type.
Booking CodeA unique identifier assigned to each booking for tracking and reference purposes.
DROPA one-way trip type where the driver drops the passenger and immediately ends the booking.
WAITINGA trip type where the driver waits at the destination for the passenger before returning. Duration is specified in minutes.
EPSEmployee Passenger Services — the internal fleet management program at PT Vale Indonesia.
Fleet MapA live map showing the GPS positions of all company taxis and their current routes.
Gantt CalendarA visual timeline view where each row is a taxi and each block is a booking, arranged along a time axis.
GPS ActiveA driver whose location has been updated within the last 10 minutes. Shown with a green satellite icon on the fleet map.
GPS StaleA driver whose location has not updated for more than 10 minutes — the last known position is shown at reduced opacity.
OpenRouteServiceThe mapping API used to calculate road-following routes between two GPS coordinates. Routes follow actual roads, not straight lines.
PWAProgressive Web App — a web application installable on a mobile device like a native app, without going through an app store.
Push NotificationAn alert delivered to your device even when the TaxiBook EPS app is not open, powered by the browser's push service.
ReassignMoving an existing booking from one taxi/driver to another. Only coordinators can reassign trips.
Saved LocationsNamed GPS coordinates registered by the coordinator for common PT Vale site locations, selectable during booking to avoid geocoding errors.
Wake LockA browser feature that prevents the device screen from turning off during an active trip, ensuring continuous GPS tracking.
